Disaster Resource Allocation

Welcome to a straightforward yet insightful game that sheds light on the critical concept of resource allocation in disaster response scenarios.

In this engaging experience, you will work collaboratively with specially programmed agents to strategically allocate resources in the aftermath of a disaster.

Your primary objective is to save as many lives as possible while efficiently managing and minimizing the utilization of resources and communications. As you embark on this mission, you can choose a role of a manager for the agencies outlined below.

Understanding the rules outlined below will be crucial for making informed and effective decisions throughout the game.

Resources

Food: Provide nutrition for the injured people and is therefore essential for their recovery. One injured people needs one food per day.

Drink: Provide the injured people and is therefore essential for their recovery. One injured people needs one water per day.

Staff: They can take care of injured or collect food and drink. One injured people needs one staff per day. Injured people needs 5 days to recover.

Roles

Shelter: You will directly deal with injured people. You need to decide when to request for resources and how much to order.

Warehouse: You will control the distribution of food and drink to Shelter and Station.

Station: You will manage the staff and decide on the allocation of them to Shelter or Warehouse.

Kitchen: You will directly deal with injured people. You need to decide when to request for resources and how much to order.

Clinic: You will control the distribution of food and drink to Shelter and Station.

Volunteers: You will manage the staff and decide on the allocation of them to Shelter or Warehouse.
